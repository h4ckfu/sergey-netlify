I specifically remember when I knew that public education was bullshit. It's Nathaniel Hawthorne's fault...

I never thrived in the public education system of the eighties but I quit trying all together when I was forced to read “The House of the Seven Gables” and told it was a classic and great literature.

While some of my classmates had never read a book outside of school, I had already read extensively by this point. For at least a few years books were my drug of choice.

I would dodge my cretinous contemporaries and their ridiculous posing and primping by grabbing a good book and spending lunch reading wherever I was least likely to be found.

While I didn’t always do everything I was assigned in School nobody ever had to force me to read, especially a “classic”. Sure I’d read about Holden Caulfield; but I had also devoured A Brave New World, Animal Farm, and even Beyond Good and evil by the time I was force fed this drivel.
